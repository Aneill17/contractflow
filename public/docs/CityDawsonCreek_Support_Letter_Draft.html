Re: Letter of Support — Elias Range Stays Healthcare Workforce Housing Hub, Dawson Creek, BC

To the Natural Resources Canada Grant Review Committee,

The City of Dawson Creek is pleased to offer its support for the proposed Elias Range Stays Healthcare Workforce Housing Hub, located at 10620 10th Street, Dawson Creek, British Columbia.

Dawson Creek is at an important moment. The $378 million Dawson Creek & District Hospital project represents one of the largest infrastructure investments in the city's history. It brings with it a sustained influx of skilled healthcare and construction workers — many of whom are traveling from outside the region and require accommodation for the duration of their work. The capacity to house this workforce is not a secondary concern. It is a precondition for the project's success and for the long-term stability of healthcare services in our community.

The housing gap for traveling healthcare workers in Northern BC is real and well-documented. Northern Health, which operates our regional hospital and a network of care facilities across the north, has consistently identified housing as one of the primary barriers to recruiting and retaining temporary and traveling staff in communities like ours. When healthcare workers cannot find adequate, affordable accommodation, placements go unfilled — and community health services are the first to feel it.

The proposed Healthcare Housing Hub directly addresses this gap. It provides Dawson Creek with permanent, purpose-built residential infrastructure — professionally managed, community-embedded, and designed to serve the healthcare and construction workforce that our city's growth demands. This is not a speculative development. Elias Range Stays has demonstrated, through four years of operation and established relationships with Northern Health, that it has the experience and commitment to deliver and operate this kind of facility at a high standard.

The City of Dawson Creek supports this project and the vision it represents. Investment in workforce housing is investment in community health, in local economic stability, and in Dawson Creek's capacity to attract the skilled workers essential to our future.

We encourage the Natural Resources Canada grant committee to give this application its full consideration.
