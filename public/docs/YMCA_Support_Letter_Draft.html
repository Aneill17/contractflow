Re: Letter of Support — Elias Range Stays Healthcare Workforce Housing Hub, Dawson Creek, BC

To the Natural Resources Canada Grant Review Committee,

The YMCA of [Region] is pleased to offer this letter of support for the proposed Elias Range Stays Healthcare Workforce Housing Hub in Dawson Creek, British Columbia.

Access to quality childcare is one of the most significant barriers facing families considering employment in rural and remote communities. For traveling and relocating healthcare workers — nurses, physicians, allied health professionals — the availability of trusted, nearby childcare is often the deciding factor in whether a placement is accepted or declined. In communities like Dawson Creek, where Northern Health relies heavily on incoming staff to maintain service levels, this barrier has a direct and measurable impact on the health system's ability to serve the region.

The proposed Healthcare Housing Hub represents an opportunity to address this gap in a coordinated, community-embedded way. We have had preliminary conversations with Elias Range Stays about the potential for childcare access and integration as part of the hub's broader community services — an alignment that reflects our shared belief that workforce stability and family support are inseparable.

Purpose-built housing for healthcare workers — professionally managed, community-connected, and designed to reduce isolation — is exactly the kind of infrastructure that makes rural communities viable for the families these communities depend on. We believe this project would make a meaningful difference in Dawson Creek's ability to attract and retain the healthcare workforce Northern BC needs.

We support the development of this project and the vision it represents: that people who come to serve our communities deserve to live and raise their families in environments that are stable, connected, and well-supported.

We encourage the committee to give this application its full consideration.

Simon Adams
Executive Director
